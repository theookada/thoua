Die Rolls

Die Rolls are used by Characters to resolve their actions. Die Rolls in this system are typically opposed and the difference determines the success or failure of the used Ability. Die Rolls fall under either Attacker Rolls or Defender Rolls.

Attacker Rolls

Attacker Rolls are primarily used when a Character uses an Ability on an Enemy. Attacker Rolls include Attack and Status Rolls. Attack Rolls represent physical attacks and add STR. Status Rolls represent mental attacks and add INT. Attacker Rolls use the Die or Dice listed in the Ability's entry (Ability Die) and appropriate Attribute to determine the total.

Attack Rolls = Ability Die + STR

Status Rolls = Ability Die + INT

Defender Rolls

Defender Rolls are primarily used when a Character is being targeted by an Enemy’s Ability. Defender Rolls include Defense and Resistance Rolls. Defense Rolls represent physical defense and add VIT. Resistance Rolls represent mental defense and add WIS. Defender Rolls use the Die or Dice based on the Character’s EM Total (see Elemental Map) and appropriate Attribute to determine the total.

Defense Rolls = EM Die + VIT

Resistance Rolls = EM Die + WIS

Opposed Rolls

As mentioned before the Die Rolls in this system are typically opposed to determine how much damage is done or if an ability is successful. See the following to determine which Attacker and Defender Rolls are opposed.

Attack Rolls are opposed by Defense Rolls

Status Rolls are opposed by Resistance Rolls

For more see Combat

Unopposed Rolls

Certain Abilities may list an Effect that includes a Die Roll without listing the type of Die Roll used. In these cases, use the Die Roll plus bonus listed in the entry. Examples of Unopposed Rolls are Heal 1d8+2 and At the End of the Character's Turn, Target takes Damage 1d4+1
